How to Use Your GiftCardMall MyGift Card Online Without Getting Declined

The fix is simpler than you think — most declines have one cause and one solution.

✅ Quick answer — do this first

  1. Register your card at giftcards.com with your name and home address
  2. At checkout, enter the exact same address as your billing address
  3. For ZIP-only fields, use just the ZIP code of your registered address
  4. Check your balance first to make sure it covers the full purchase amount

Why does my card get declined online?

Online merchants use a security system called Address Verification Service (AVS). When you enter a card number online, the merchant checks that the billing address you provide matches what's registered with the card issuer.

With most regular credit and debit cards, this happens automatically. With a prepaid gift card like the GiftCardMall MyGift Visa or Mastercard, there's an extra step — you have to register your billing address yourself before you can use it online.

If you skip this step, the card issuer has no address on file. The AVS check fails. The transaction gets declined — even if you have plenty of balance.

⚠️ Most common mistake

Trying to use the card online before registering it. Registration is required for online purchases — without it, almost every online merchant will decline your card.

Step-by-step: how to set up your card for online use

  1. Go to giftcards.com — this is the official site that replaced GiftCardMall in 2025. The old URL (mygift.giftcardmall.com) redirects here.
  2. Create a free account or log in if you already have one.
  3. Add your card — enter your 16-digit card number, expiration date, and CVV.
  4. Enter your billing address — use your real home address. This is the address you'll need to enter at online checkouts.
  5. Save your card — registration is complete. Your card is now ready for online use.

💡 Pro tip

Write down or screenshot the exact address format you used during registration. Use the exact same format — same abbreviations, same ZIP code — at every online checkout. Even small differences can cause an AVS mismatch and get your card declined.

How to use your card at specific merchants

🛒

Amazon

Go to Account → Payment methods → Add a debit or credit card. Enter your card details and registered billing address.

Make sure your balance covers the full order amount — Amazon rarely allows partial gift card payments.
💙

PayPal

Go to Wallet → Link a card → enter your card details and billing address. Once linked, use PayPal at checkout.

PayPal is the best workaround — once linked, it bypasses AVS issues at most merchants.
🏪

Walmart

Enter card details at checkout and use your registered billing address. Walmart accepts prepaid Visa and Mastercard cards.

If declined, try adding the card to your Walmart account first before checking out.
🎯

Target

Add your card during checkout. Enter card number, expiry, CVV, and your registered billing address exactly as registered.

Target sometimes requires the full billing address — not just ZIP code.
🏷️

eBay

Add the card as a payment method in your account settings first. Then select it at checkout with your registered billing address.

Adding to PayPal first and paying via PayPal on eBay is more reliable.
🌐

Other online stores

The process is the same everywhere: enter card details + registered billing address. If declined, use the PayPal method.

When only ZIP code is asked, use the ZIP from your registered address.

The PayPal method — best workaround

If you keep getting declined at specific merchants, the most reliable workaround is to link your GiftCardMall MyGift card to PayPal and pay with PayPal instead.

  1. Log into PayPal at paypal.com or open the PayPal app
  2. Go to Wallet → "Link a card"
  3. Enter your card details — card number, expiry, CVV, and registered billing address
  4. Confirm the card — PayPal may charge a small temporary authorization (usually $1) that is immediately reversed
  5. At checkout, select PayPal as your payment method and choose your MyGift card from your PayPal wallet

ℹ️ Why PayPal works better

When you pay with PayPal, the merchant only sees PayPal — not your gift card details. PayPal handles the AVS verification on their end, which is more lenient and accepts prepaid cards that direct merchant checkout often rejects.

Other reasons your card might be declined online

If you've registered your card and still get declined, here are other possible causes:

💰

Insufficient balance

Your card balance is less than the total purchase amount including tax and shipping. Check your balance at giftcards.com first.

🌍

International merchant

GiftCardMall MyGift cards are for US use only. If the merchant's payment processor is based outside the US, the card may be declined.

🔄

Subscription services

Some subscription services (Netflix, Spotify) do not accept prepaid cards. This is a merchant-level restriction, not a card issue.
