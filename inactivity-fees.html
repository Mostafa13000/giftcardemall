GiftCardMall Inactivity Fees
— How to Avoid Losing Your Balance

Your GiftCardMall MyGift card balance can disappear slowly due to monthly fees. Here's exactly how it works and how to stop it.

⚠️

Your balance can drain to zero — even if you never use the card

After 12 months of no purchases, GiftCardMall MyGift cards are charged a monthly fee that slowly eats your balance. Many cardholders don't find out until it's too late.

How GiftCardMall inactivity fees work

GiftCardMall MyGift Visa and Mastercard prepaid cards include a dormancy fee in their cardholder agreement. This fee is legal under US federal law and is disclosed at the time of purchase — but many people miss it or forget about it.

Here's the exact breakdown of how the fee works:

Fee breakdown

Fee name Monthly Inactivity / Dormancy Fee
Fee amount $2.95 per month
When it starts After 12 consecutive months with no purchases
How it's charged Automatically deducted from your remaining balance
When it stops When you make a purchase OR balance hits $0
Can balance go negative? No — stops at zero

⏱️ Real example

You receive a $50 gift card and forget about it for 18 months. After month 12, $2.95 gets deducted every month. By month 18 you've lost $17.70 — over a third of your card's value — without ever spending a dollar.

Two scenarios — spot the difference

❌ What happens if you forget

$100 card. No purchases for 14 months. Month 13: -$2.95. Month 14: -$2.95. You've lost $5.90 before you even tried to use it. If you forget for 3 years, the card is at $0.

✅ What happens if you act

$100 card. You make a $1 Amazon purchase at month 11. The clock resets. No fees charged. Your $99 balance is fully protected for another 12 months.

How to avoid inactivity fees — 4 methods

  1. Use it within 12 months. The simplest fix. Make any purchase — even $1 — within the first year of receiving the card. This resets the inactivity clock completely.
  2. Set a calendar reminder. When you receive the card, set a reminder for 11 months later. That gives you one month to make a purchase before the fees kick in.
  3. Register the card at giftcards.com. Registered cards make it easier to track your balance and transaction history — so you can see when the last purchase was made and whether fees are being charged.
  4. Spend the full balance before the deadline. If you know you won't use the card soon, spend it all at once. Even buying a small Amazon gift card or adding it to PayPal counts as a transaction.

💡 Best trick to reset the clock

Buy a $1 item on Amazon using your MyGift card as payment. This counts as a transaction and resets the 12-month inactivity period — protecting your full remaining balance.

Can you get your money back after fees?

If you've already been charged inactivity fees, it's worth calling customer service at 1-833-800-0550. While there's no guarantee, some cardholders have reported that agents reversed recent fees as a one-time courtesy — especially if the charges were within the last 1-2 billing cycles.

When you call, be polite and explain that you were unaware of the fee. Ask specifically: "Can you reverse the inactivity fees on my account as a one-time courtesy?" The worst they can say is no.

⚠️ Important: Act quickly

The longer you wait, the less likely a reversal is. Call as soon as you notice fees on your account. Check your transaction history at giftcards.com to see exactly when fees started.
